About Me

Hello, I'm Ronan Armstrong, multimedia specialist and musician. Please feel free to look through this resume, and reach out to me with any questions you may have.

Recording

Production

Since I was 5, I have been engrossed with writing and producing songs for both myself and my friends and family. I started taking classical composition lessons with Dr. Sarah Miller, and continued studying a variety of styles under her until I was 18.

I became interested in modern music production after I was introduced to Logic Pro X by a friend of mine when I was 14. Since then, I've produced a wide selection of music, including contemporary pop, hip-hop and R&B, EDM, 80's rock (experimental and glam), synthwave, and many more.

My strong foundation in classical technique has given me a unique perspective and approach to popular music, and I try to push myself to my limit in every project I undertake.

Engineering

Shortly after I started using Logic Pro X, I became fascinated with the technical aspects of the tools used in music production. While I studied on my own for a few years, it wasn't until I attended McNally Smith College of Music in 2017 that I learned how large the technology world actually was. It was there that I began learning about the many aspects of both analog and digital recording, and when McNally Smith closed unexpectedly in December of 2017, I pushed myself harder than ever to learn as much as possible, pulling from contacts I made at McNally Smith and the many master classes and user manuals available on the internet.

Since I started studying music engineering, I've familiarized myself with many of the most popular pieces of equipment and software used in studio enviroments, including a variety of recording consoles, patchbays, and DAWs/plugins. After buying an analog recording console in the spring of 2018, I took it upon myself to hand wire a patchbay system, including both full and half normal connections, solder-type and ADC punchdown, and over a half mile of cable from Redco.

Coding

Front End

I've been designing and coding user interfaces since I was 12 years old, and have had a significant amount of experience in creating user friendly, accessible interfaces for a variety of clients including Strom Aviation, and a variety of intranet systems.

Within interface design, my primary focus has been responsive and accessible design. The majority of web traffic since 2016 has come from a mobile device, and I believe creating a clean interface optimized for low bandwidth connections is crucial in today's web design landscape. To achieve this, I prioritize efficient code coupled with clean and responsive CSS over primarily JavaScript based solutions.

Back End

To complement my experience designing user interfaces, I started designing back end systems in 2016. I've worked with a variety of back end languages ranging from classic ASP to Meteor.js. As a designer, my main focus is on efficient data retrieval and secure data storage. I've worked extensively with hashing/salting sensitive information, as well as a variety of other data security techniques.

While my specialty is front end design, I think a clean user interface must be able to interact in a logical and efficient way with the back end, which is why I've learned a variety of database systems, including SQL, Access, MySQL, and MongoDB. I'm also familiar with database structure, including key-value pair systems and NoSQL.
