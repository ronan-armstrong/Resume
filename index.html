About Me

Hello, I'm Ronan Armstrong, multimedia specialist and musician. Please feel free to look through this resume, and reach out to me with any questions you may have.

Recording

Production

Since I was 5, I have been engrossed with writing and producing songs for both myself and my friends and family. I started taking classical composition lessons with Dr. Sarah Miller, and continued studying a variety of styles under her until I was 18.

I became interested in modern music production after I was introduced to Logic Pro X by a friend of mine when I was 14. Since then, I've produced a wide selection of music, including contemporary pop, hip-hop and R&B, EDM, 80's rock (experimental and glam), synthwave, and many more.

My strong foundation in classical technique has given me a unique perspective and approach to popular music, and I try to push myself to my limit in every project I undertake.

Engineering

Shortly after I started using Logic Pro X, I became fascinated with the technical aspects of the tools used in music production. While I studied on my own for a few years, it wasn't until I attended McNally Smith College of Music in 2017 that I learned how large the technology world actually was. It was there that I began learning about the many aspects of both analog and digital recording, and when McNally Smith closed unexpectedly in December of 2017, I pushed myself harder than ever to learn as much as possible, pulling from contacts I made at McNally Smith and the many master classes and user manuals available on the internet.

Since I started studying music engineering, I've familiarized myself with many of the most popular pieces of equipment and software used in studio enviroments, including a variety of recording consoles, patchbays, and DAWs/plugins.
